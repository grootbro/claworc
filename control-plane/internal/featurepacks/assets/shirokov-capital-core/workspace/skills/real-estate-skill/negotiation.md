# Negotiation Guide

## Offer Strategy

### Pricing Your Offer

**Never base on asking price.** Base on:
- recent comparable sales
- days on market
- price reductions
- condition relative to comps

**Positioning:**

| Market | Strategy |
|--------|----------|
| Hot | At or above asking, strong terms |
| Balanced | 3-5% below, reasonable terms |
| Slow | 10%+ below, test seller motivation |

### Earnest Money Signaling

| Amount | Signal |
|--------|--------|
| 1% | Minimum acceptable |
| 2-3% | Serious buyer |
| 5%+ | Very strong offer |

## Contingency Strategy

### Inspection Contingency

Don't waive it casually. You can still make it attractive by:
- shortening the period
- limiting small repair asks
- using pre-inspection when appropriate

### Appraisal Contingency

If appraisal comes in low:
1. challenge the appraisal
2. split the difference
3. pay the difference in cash
4. renegotiate
5. walk away

### Financing Contingency

Sellers prefer:
- pre-approved over pre-qualified
- conventional over weaker financing profiles
- stronger down payment profiles
- local lenders with faster communication

## Repair Negotiations

Prioritize:
1. safety issues
2. structural and major systems
3. lender-required fixes
4. material deferred maintenance

Avoid focusing on purely cosmetic items.

## Difficult Conversations

### Price Reduction

Use data, not pressure:

"Based on market feedback and comparable activity, the more competitive path is X. This should improve the chance of a stronger offer set."

### Buyer Backing Out

Stay factual:

"The inspection uncovered issues beyond our comfort level for this scenario, so we're exercising our contingency rights."

### Multiple Offer Situation

Use escalation clauses only when the client is genuinely prepared to pay the maximum number.
